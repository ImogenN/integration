"Therapists practise in all walks of life and all parts of society, from NHS clinics to workplaces and education. They are trained to deal with a range of situations, helping people to cope with such issues as anxiety and bereavement, relationship difficulties, sexual and racial issues, child abuse and trauma, or personal problem solving.

What is Integrative Counselling?

Integrative therapy, or integrative counselling is a combined approach to psychotherapy that brings together different elements of specific therapies. Integrative therapists take the view that there is no single approach that can treat each client in all situations.

Each person needs to be considered as a whole and counselling techniques must be tailored to their individual needs and personal circumstances. My style of integrative counselling incorporates person-centered counselling, cognitive behavioural therapy, psychodynamic approaches and solution focussed models.

This range of tools and techniques are utilised tailored to your style of learning and personal development, integrating affective, behavioural, cognitive, and physiological aspects of your psyche.

Group Faciliation Services

Group facilitation involves assisting parties to co-create a constructive dialogue. Facilitators help groups to set goals, negotiate boundaries and manage discussion to maximise utility of the session for the whole. For example, facilitators help group members to identify difficulties they have with problem solving, interpersonal communication and teamwork.

The objective of group facilitation is to promote understanding between the parties involved. For more goal oriented group settings, facilitators may set an agenda proposing a series of manageable targets to help retain group focus and reach pre-agreed objectives. I am available to work with both commercial and therapeutically focussed groups. Contact me to discuss your group facilitation needs, and we can discuss if this service is suited to your requirements.
